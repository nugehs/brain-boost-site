No nagging, no negotiating. The deal is built into the laptop itself.

FAQ

Honest answers to fair questions

Will this stop a determined teenager?

Honestly? No — and we'd rather tell you that than sell you a fantasy. Brain Boost Buddy is a strong daily routine-builder, not an uncrackable vault. A determined teenager with admin rights on the machine can force-quit it.

For most children it works because the path of least resistance is simply doing the questions. If you need hard enforcement, Brain Boost Buddy pairs well with your operating system's built-in Screen Time controls.

Is it safe to install on my child's laptop?

The macOS app is signed and notarized by Apple, which means Apple has checked it for malicious software before it ever reaches your machine. The Windows version comes as a standard installer. There's no tracking and no account required.

How do I stop or remove it later?

Any time you like: open the gate, choose Parent unlock, enter your PIN, and press Switch off Brain Boost Buddy. The gate stops locking the laptop and stops opening at login — you can then drag the app to the Trash, or switch it back on later. Your licence stays valid either way.

And until you've finished the first-time parent setup, the app is just a normal quittable app — it never locks anything before you've set your PIN.

What age is it for?

It's built around the UK Key Stage 2 curriculum for ages 9–11, with three difficulty levels: Year 5, Year 6 and Greater Depth. The Maths topics also cover common 11+ ground, and English follows the UK statutory spelling lists.

What happens if my child gets stuck and can't pass?

A wrong answer is never a dead end — the app shows a friendly mini-lesson explaining the method, then a fresh question to retry. If they run out of tries, your parent PIN can grant another try, drop the difficulty, lower the number of questions, skip today entirely, or just unlock the laptop. Nobody is ever locked out of their own computer — you hold the key.

Won't they just memorise the answers?

No — questions are procedurally generated, so the numbers and words change every single time. There's no fixed question bank to memorise or share.
